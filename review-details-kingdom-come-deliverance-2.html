The Review

The first Kingdom Come was rough in a lot of very specific, very forgivable ways for a small studio's debut. The sequel has a much bigger budget and it shows in basically every system — combat, world density, and technical stability all took a real step forward.

Combat Finally Feels Fair

The original's combat was notorious for being obtuse even to players who wanted to like it. This version keeps the same commitment to realistic, skill-based swordplay but does a much better job teaching it, and it's more forgiving without losing what made it interesting in the first place.

A Denser, More Reactive Bohemia

NPCs actually track your reputation and past actions in ways that feel meaningful rather than cosmetic. Side quests frequently branch based on how you've handled previous situations, which gives the world a sense of consequence a lot of open-world RPGs only pretend to have.

The Verdict

This is the sequel the original deserved — same commitment to grounded, unglamorous medieval RPG mechanics, but built on a foundation that finally supports the ambition. Less jank, more game.

Pros

  • Combat is deeper and much better taught than the original
  • World reacts to your choices in tangible ways
  • Huge improvement in technical stability
  • Strong sense of place and historical grounding

Cons

  • Still a slow burn that won't suit action-RPG fans
  • Some inventory and menu systems feel dated
  • Horse and travel pacing can drag
  • Steep learning curve for newcomers to the series
