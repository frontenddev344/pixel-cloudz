The Review

Wilds is the biggest structural shift the series has made since World, dropping the zone-based map loading in favor of fully seamless regions with dynamic weather that changes how monsters behave. It's an ambitious swing that mostly lands.

Weather Isn't Just Set Dressing

Storms, droughts, and other weather events actively change monster behavior, migration, and even which materials are available to gather. It gives the world a sense of being alive in a way previous entries only gestured at.

Fourteen Weapons, Still Deep

The full weapon roster returns essentially intact, and the game does a better job than most prior entries at onboarding people into the notoriously deep weapon-specific combat systems, without watering any of them down for veterans.

Bottom Line

A genuinely ambitious evolution of the formula that occasionally trips over its own scope — some performance hitches, some AI companion quirks — but the hunting itself is as good as it's ever been.

Pros
  • Seamless open regions are a real structural improvement
  • Dynamic weather meaningfully affects hunts
  • Full 14-weapon roster with better onboarding
  • Strong monster design and fight variety
Cons
  • Performance can dip in the busier open areas
  • AI hunting companions have some pathing quirks
  • Some monsters feel recycled from prior entries
  • Multiplayer session setup still has minor friction
