About PixelCloudz

How This Started

A Few People Tired Of Inflated Scores

PixelCloudz started because a handful of us kept noticing the same thing: review scores that read more like advertising than an actual opinion. Every big release seemed to land somewhere between an 8 and a 10, regardless of whether it ran, whether it was finished, or whether anyone on staff had actually played past the tutorial.

So we started writing the reviews we wished existed — the kind that tell you a game stutters on base consoles, or that a "day one patch" is doing a lot of heavy lifting, or that the AAA sequel everyone's hyping is honestly just fine. We cover new releases across every major platform now, but the goal hasn't changed: tell you what you're actually getting before you spend the money.

What We're Trying To Do

Tell you what a game actually is before you buy it — how it runs, what's missing, and whether it's worth your specific platform and budget. Not what a press kit says it is.

Where We'd Like This To Go

A site where our score actually means something — where a 9 means "buy this" and a 6 means "wait for a sale," instead of everything landing somewhere around 8.
