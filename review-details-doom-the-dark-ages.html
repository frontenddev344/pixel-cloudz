The Review

The Dark Ages is a real gear shift from 2016 and Eternal's fast, aerial combat, and it takes a bit to adjust if you came in expecting more of the same. This is a heavier, more grounded Doom, built around a shield and standing your ground instead of never touching the floor.

The Shield Changes The Whole Rhythm

Parrying and shield-throwing become as central to combat as the guns themselves, and it works — timing a parry into a big melee opening feels great, and the game throws enough variety of enemy types at you that the shield never feels like a one-note gimmick.

Bigger, Slower, Still Metal

Giant mech and dragon-riding sections punctuate the more grounded shield combat, giving the campaign a real sense of scale escalation. It's a deliberate trade of the previous games' speed for weight and impact, and mostly that trade pays off.

So, Is It Worth It

Fans expecting Eternal 2 might need a minute to recalibrate, but taken on its own terms this is a confident, weighty shooter that does a lot with its shield-first combat identity.

Pros
  • Shield-parry combat adds a satisfying new rhythm
  • Strong scale escalation with mech and dragon sections
  • Still has that unmistakable Doom aggression
  • Excellent sound design and weapon feedback
Cons
  • Slower pace will disappoint some Eternal fans
  • Story cutscenes interrupt momentum more than prior entries
  • A few enemy encounters lean too heavily on shield-bash spam
  • Level variety dips in the midgame
