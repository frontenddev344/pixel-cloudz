The Review

A decade on, The Witcher 3 is still what gets brought up whenever someone asks what open-world RPG storytelling should look like. The current-gen update in 2022 gave it another visual pass, but the writing was always the real draw.

Side Quests With Actual Stakes

This is still one of the only open-world games where side content regularly rivals the main story in quality — the Bloody Baron questline alone is better writing than most full RPGs manage across their entire runtime.

Geralt, Gwent, And Everything In Between

Beyond the main narrative, Gwent (the in-game card game) got popular enough to spin off into its own standalone title, which says something about how well-built even the game's side systems are.

The Takeaway

If you've somehow avoided this for a decade, the current-gen update is the version to play. Dense, mature, confident storytelling that most open-world RPGs still haven't caught up to.

Pros

  • Side quest writing rivals or beats the main story
  • Blood and Wine and Hearts of Stone remain some of the best DLC ever made
  • Current-gen update improves visuals and load times significantly
  • Gwent adds genuine depth as an optional side system

Cons

  • Combat feels dated compared to modern action-RPGs
  • Inventory and menu management can be clunky
  • Horse controls (Roach) are a long-running community joke for a reason
  • Main story pacing dips in the middle third
