The Review

Hollow Knight is the game a lot of modern metroidvanias are still quietly compared against, and it earns that reputation through sheer density — a hand-drawn world this large, built by a genuinely small team, still doesn't feel real.

A Map That Makes You Work For It

You don't get map information for free — you have to find the cartographer in each area and buy the map, then manually mark locations yourself. It sounds like a small inconvenience but it completely changes how exploration feels versus a game that hands you everything.

Bosses That Escalate Honestly

Difficulty ramps up fairly and consistently, and several late-game optional bosses are genuinely some of the hardest, most satisfying fights in the genre once you learn their patterns. Nothing here feels like padding.

In The End

Years later, this remains a genre-defining release, and at its price point there's very little reason not to have played it if you have any interest in metroidvanias at all.

Pros

  • Exceptional hand-drawn art and animation throughout
  • Manual mapping system makes exploration feel earned
  • Boss difficulty escalates fairly and rewards patience
  • Enormous amount of content for the asking price

Cons

  • Map system's learning curve frustrates some newcomers
  • A few areas require significant backtracking
  • Some optional bosses are extremely difficult
  • Lore is almost entirely environmental and easy to miss
