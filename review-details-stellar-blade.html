The Review

Stellar Blade's combat takes a minute to click, and once it does, it's genuinely satisfying — parry timing is tight, tighter than a lot of games that lean this hard on it, and enemy attacks are readable enough that failure feels like your fault, not the game's.

Combat Rewards Patience

Perfect parries (called Beta Counters here) trigger a slow-motion counter that's as useful mechanically as it is satisfying visually. The skill tree gives you real choice in how aggressive or defensive you want your build to lean, which keeps combat from getting stale over a 20+ hour campaign.

A Linear Structure That Knows What It's Doing

This isn't trying to be an open world game, and it's better for it — the semi-linear structure lets the team focus level design and pacing instead of padding runtime with busywork. Exploration off the critical path is rewarded but never mandatory.

Wrapping Up

A confident, well-built action game from a first-time console developer, held back slightly by a story that leans on familiar beats. If parry-based combat is your thing, this delivers consistently across its whole runtime.

Pros

  • Skill tree gives real build flexibility
  • Linear structure keeps pacing tight
  • Strong art direction and character animation

  • PS5 console exclusive
  • Some environments feel visually repetitive
