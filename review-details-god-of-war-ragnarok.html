The Review

Wrapping up a two-part story this ambitious is genuinely difficult, and Ragnarök manages it without the sense of deflation a lot of concluding chapters carry. This closes out the Norse saga started in 2018 while still finding room to expand the cast meaningfully.

A Bigger Cast, Handled Well

More playable sections away from Kratos, expanded time with Atreus, and a deeper supporting cast all get real development time here, and none of it feels like padding — every perspective shift serves the larger story.

Combat Refined, Not Reinvented

The Leviathan Axe and Blades of Chaos combat from 2018 gets meaningful new tools and a genuinely excellent new axe-throw recall mechanic, without needing to reinvent what already worked so well.

The Long And Short Of It

A satisfying, emotionally earned conclusion to the Norse era of this series, with combat, presentation, and pacing that rarely lets up across a long campaign. One of the best PS5 exclusives available.

Pros

  • Sticks the landing on a genuinely ambitious two-game arc
  • Expanded cast gets real, earned development
  • Combat refinements feel meaningful without losing what worked
  • Presentation and voice acting remain best-in-class

Cons

  • PS5/PS4 console exclusive limits availability
  • Some side content (favors) feels less essential than the main story
  • A few pacing lulls in the midgame
  • Puzzle design occasionally overstays individual sections
