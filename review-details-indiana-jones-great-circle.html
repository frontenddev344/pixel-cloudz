The Review

MachineGames built its name on Wolfenstein, which makes it a slightly odd fit for Indiana Jones on paper — and yet this might be the best licensed adventure game in years. It gets the tone exactly right: pulpy, funny, occasionally genuinely tense.

First-Person Was The Right Call

Puzzle-solving, exploration, and even the fistfights all benefit from the first-person perspective in ways that would've been lost in third-person. Combat leans more toward improvised, stealth-first problem solving than straightforward shooting, which fits the character.

Troy Baker Disappears Into The Role

Playing as a digital Harrison Ford could've been distracting or uncanny, but the performance capture and voice work make it work almost immediately. Side conversations with NPCs across the game's globe-trotting locations are genuinely funny in a way licensed games rarely manage.

Closing Thoughts

A confident, well-crafted adventure that understands exactly what makes Indiana Jones fun and builds a game around it instead of just slapping the license onto a generic template. One of the better surprises of the year.

Pros
  • Nails the tone and humor of the films
  • First-person perspective elevates puzzles and exploration
  • Strong voice work and performance capture
  • Globe-trotting structure keeps locations varied
Cons
  • Combat can feel thin if you rely on direct confrontation
  • Some puzzle solutions aren't clearly telegraphed
  • PS5 version arrived months after PC/Xbox
  • A few areas encourage more backtracking than needed
