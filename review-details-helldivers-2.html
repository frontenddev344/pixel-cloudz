The Review

Nobody really expected a satirical third-person co-op shooter to become one of the biggest games of 2024, but here we are. Helldivers 2 takes the top-down chaos of the original and turns it into a genuinely great shooter, while keeping every bit of the over-the-top propaganda satire intact.

Friendly Fire Is A Feature, Not A Bug

You will be killed by your own team more than you'd like to admit, and that's kind of the point — it forces communication and creates the kind of chaotic stories that make this game so shareable. Stratagems (call-in weapons and support items) add a layer of coordination most co-op shooters don't bother with.

A Live Game That's Actually Been Handled Well

Post-launch, Arrowhead has generally been responsive to balance complaints and community feedback, walking back a few controversial nerfs after backlash. The evolving galactic war narrative gives you a reason to log back in beyond just grinding for gear.

Final Verdict

This is one of the rare live-service shooters that actually earns the description "more fun with friends" instead of just marketing it. Bring two others, expect chaos, and don't get too attached to your Helldiver.

Pros
  • Genuinely funny satire that never undercuts the gameplay
  • Co-op chaos creates memorable moments constantly
  • Strong post-launch support and balance responsiveness
  • Stratagem system adds real tactical depth
Cons
  • Friendly fire frustrates players who don't enjoy chaos
  • Balance patches have occasionally overcorrected
  • Solo play is noticeably weaker than co-op
  • Matchmaking can be inconsistent during peak content drops
