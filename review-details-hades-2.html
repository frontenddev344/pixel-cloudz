The Review

Following up a game as beloved as the original Hades is a genuinely difficult task, and Supergiant's answer — even still in Early Access — is a sequel that expands the combat and structure without losing what made the first game special.

Melinoe Plays Very Differently From Zagreus

The new protagonist's kit revolves around dual-wielded weapons and a stronger emphasis on spellcasting, which gives combat a noticeably different rhythm from the original even though the core boon-and-run structure is familiar.

Early Access, But Barely Feels Like It

Even in its unfinished state, the amount of content, voice acting, and polish already present is more than a lot of full-price finished roguelikes ship with. Supergiant has a strong track record of using Early Access well, and this is following that pattern so far.

Summing It Up

Already excellent even before its full release, with combat that feels distinct enough from the original to justify its own existence rather than just being reskinned content.

Pros

  • Melinoe's combat kit feels distinct from the original protagonist
  • Exceptional voice acting and writing, even in Early Access
  • Run structure remains addictive and well-paced
  • Supergiant's Early Access track record inspires confidence

Cons

  • Still incomplete — some systems will change before full release
  • Fewer boons/content than the fully-released original, for now
  • Switch version performance can dip in busier fights
  • Some balance still being actively tuned
