The Review

It's strange to talk about Cyberpunk 2077 in 2026 without mentioning the launch, but honestly the game people are playing now barely resembles the broken mess that shipped in 2020. Multiple years of patches and the Phantom Liberty expansion turned this into one of the better open-world RPGs on the market, which makes the whole saga kind of remarkable.

Night City Is Still The Best Part

The city itself remains the strongest argument for playing this. Vertical, dense, loud in a way that most open worlds aren't willing to commit to. Every district has its own visual identity, and just riding through on a bike taking in the neon and traffic noise is still enjoyable years later.

The RPG Systems Finally Feel Finished

Police AI, cyberware, the perk trees — all of it got rebuilt in the 2.0 update alongside Phantom Liberty, and it shows. Combat has actual weight now, hacking feels like a real build path instead of a gimmick, and driving controls significantly less like a shopping cart.

Where We Landed

If you bounced off this at launch, it's genuinely worth a second look now. The writing was always good; the game underneath it just wasn't ready. It is now, and Phantom Liberty might be the best DLC CD Projekt has ever shipped.

Pros

  • Night City is dense and genuinely fun to just exist in
  • Phantom Liberty is a legitimately great expansion
  • Combat and driving feel worlds better post-2.0
  • V's story and companions still hit hard

Cons

  • Still carries reputation baggage from a rough launch
  • Some side content feels dated compared to the main story
  • Base last-gen consoles show their age
  • Police system, while improved, still has odd edges
