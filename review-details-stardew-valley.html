The Review

One developer built this entire game — art, code, music, writing — and a decade later it's still receiving free major content updates. That's a genuinely wild thing to type about a $15 game, and it's part of why Stardew Valley still gets recommended constantly.

There's Always Something Else To Do

Farming is just the entry point — fishing, mining, combat in the mines, relationship building with an enormous cast of townsfolk, and now with recent updates, entirely new areas and endgame content that didn't exist at launch.

Endlessly Cozy, Never Shallow

The low-pressure pace makes it easy to pick up for twenty minutes or lose an entire evening to, and multiplayer co-op farming with friends adds a completely different dynamic to the same core loop.

What We Think

This is as close to a universal recommendation as we make. Whether you want a relaxing time-sink or a surprisingly deep simulation game, Stardew Valley delivers on both fronts for less than the price of a fast food meal.

Pros

  • Extraordinary amount of content for a solo-developed game
  • Continues to receive free major updates a decade later
  • Low-pressure pace suits both casual and dedicated play
  • Genuinely charming writing and character relationships

Cons

  • Early game grind can feel slow before systems open up
  • Some UI elements feel dated compared to modern sims
  • Combat is the weakest part of the overall package
  • Late-game content requires a real time investment to reach
