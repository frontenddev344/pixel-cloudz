The Review

Black Myth: Wukong wears its budget on its sleeve in the best way. This is a debut game from a studio nobody outside China had heard of two years ago, and it looks and plays like something with three times the experience behind it.

The Bosses Are The Whole Show

Every major boss fight here is built around a distinct gimmick, and most of them are genuinely memorable — some as spectacle, some as pure mechanical puzzles you have to learn under pressure. The game front-loads difficulty in a way that feels closer to Sekiro than Dark Souls: precise, reactive, punishing."

Gorgeous, If Occasionally Rough

Environments pull directly from real Chinese architecture and landscapes and it shows — this might be the best-looking action game released this generation. Performance on PC can be inconsistent depending on your rig even with a decent GPU, and there were a few quest-tracking issues at launch that patches have mostly ironed out.

The Bottom Line

For a first game, this is a remarkable outing, held back slightly by some rough technical edges and a lack of build variety compared to genre veterans. Still, if you want a punishing action game that actually looks like a AAA showcase, this delivers.

Pros

  • Boss design is consistently excellent and varied
  • Among the best-looking action games available
  • Combat has real weight and precision
  • Rich, distinct visual identity drawn from real mythology

Cons

  • Limited build variety compared to Soulslike peers
  • PC performance can be inconsistent
  • Some quest tracking issues, though patched
  • Story delivery is fairly opaque without outside reading
