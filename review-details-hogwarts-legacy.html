The Review

Whatever your feelings on the extended universe around it, Hogwarts Legacy delivers on the one thing everyone actually wanted from a game like this — the castle itself is dense, detailed, and genuinely magical to walk around in for the first several hours.

The Castle Is The Star

Moving staircases, house common rooms, secret passages, class attendance — the school setting is clearly where the developer's attention and budget went, and it shows in how much more alive it feels compared to the surrounding open-world map.

The Open World Is More Familiar Territory

Once you're out past the castle grounds, the game slides into a fairly standard open-world checklist structure — collectibles, repeated bandit camp layouts, side quests of uneven quality. It's competent but noticeably less inspired than the school itself.

Net Result

A strong wish-fulfillment fantasy that peaks early with the castle and coasts somewhat on the open-world side of things. Still a good time, especially for anyone who's ever wanted to just exist as a student there.

Pros

  • Hogwarts castle itself is dense and genuinely magical
  • Spell-combat system has real satisfying variety
  • Strong character customization options
  • Class attendance and house identity add nice roleplay texture

Cons

  • Open-world content outside the castle feels generic
  • Side quest writing quality varies significantly
  • Combat difficulty balance is uneven
  • Some technical performance issues, especially at launch
