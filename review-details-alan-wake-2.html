The Review

Thirteen years is a long time to wait for a sequel, and Alan Wake 2 justifies the wait by being one of the most confident, strange, and genuinely unsettling games Remedy has ever made. This isn't a bigger, safer follow-up — it's weirder than the original in almost every direction.

Two Playable Characters, Two Very Different Games

Splitting time between FBI agent Saga Anderson and the still-trapped Alan Wake gives the game two distinct tones — Saga's sections play like a grounded detective procedural with real investigation mechanics, while Alan's sections lean into full nightmare-logic surrealism.

This Is Genuinely Scary

Remedy leaned all the way into horror this time rather than treating it as an action game with spooky elements, and it pays off — limited resources, oppressive sound design, and enemies that are genuinely unsettling to fight rather than just obstacles to clear.

Last Word

A bold, strange, confidently-written horror game that rewards patience and attention to detail. Not for players who want straightforward action, but one of the best horror narratives in years for anyone willing to meet it on its own terms.

Pros

  • Two distinct playable perspectives with different tones
  • Genuinely unsettling horror atmosphere throughout
  • Ambitious, strange narrative structure that mostly pays off
  • Exceptional sound design and live-action segments

Cons

  • Slow pace and dense narrative won't suit everyone
  • Combat is intentionally limited and can feel sparse
  • Requires familiarity with Remedy's wider narrative universe to fully click
  • Fairly demanding on hardware for full visual fidelity
